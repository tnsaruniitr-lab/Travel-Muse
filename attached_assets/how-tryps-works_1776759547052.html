How TRYPS Works: Plan a Group Trip From Invite to Settle-Up

TRYPS Blog · April 21, 2026 · 6 min read
Group trips fall apart in the planning, not the trip itself. TRYPS handles the planning so the trip can be the trip.

This is the full walkthrough — five steps, one app, no spreadsheets. From the moment you create a trip to the final expense settle-up.

The short version

TRYPS turns a group trip into five clean steps:

  1. Create the trip and text your group from inside the app.
  2. Each person sets their vibe — pace, budget, food, planning depth.
  3. Add ideas to the voting block, thumbs-up the winners.
  4. Confirmed activities flow into the day-by-day itinerary.
  5. Add expenses as they happen — TRYPS splits and settles the math.

No friend needs to download anything. The whole flow runs from one link.

What do you need before you start?

Almost nothing. You need an iPhone (TRYPS is iOS for now), a destination idea — even a rough one — and a rough date window. Setup takes about 2 minutes. You don't need flights booked, accommodation chosen, or your group fully confirmed.

TRYPS is built for the messy early stage of group travel where most trips quietly die. The earlier you bring it in, the more it does.

Step 1 Create the trip and text your group

Open TRYPS, hit new trip, set a destination and a date range. Add a cover photo so the trip feels real — pulled from your camera roll or a stock library inside the app.

Then tap Text Friends. TRYPS opens iMessage with a pre-filled invite and a join link. Your friends tap the link, land inside the trip, and can immediately see the destination, dates, and other guests. No app download required. No account creation. No "what's your email?" friction.

This is where most group-trip apps lose half the group. TRYPS doesn't.

Why iMessage

Friend groups already live in group chats. Pulling them into a separate app to start coordinating is the friction point. TRYPS meets the group where it already is — then quietly takes over the planning that the chat was never going to handle anyway.

Step 2 Set your group's vibe

Once friends join, each person answers six short prompts. The vibe questionnaire is the bit that makes everything downstream smarter.

The six dimensions: how packed the days should be (Chill & Slow vs. Packed Schedule), budget style (Keep it Cheap vs. Treat Ourselves), food priority (Easy Meals vs. Foodie), group dynamic (Together vs. Split Up), activity type (Relax vs. Adventure), and how much planning the group wants to do upfront.

TRYPS uses the overlap — not the average — to surface activities that actually fit the group. The "Save as default" option carries your personal vibe across future trips so you only fill it out once.

Why this matters

Most group-trip arguments aren't about the trip itself — they're about unspoken mismatches in pace, budget, or food expectations. The vibe step takes about 90 seconds per person and makes those mismatches visible early, so the group can plan around them instead of arguing about them on day three.

Step 3 Vote on activities together

The Activities tab is where the group decides what the trip actually is. It has three zones:

  • • Confirmed — locked in. Drag activities here when the group is clearly into it. Confirmed activities flow into the itinerary automatically.
  • • Voting Block — ideas under consideration. Anyone can add an activity (paste a URL, search a place, or write it manually). Each person casts a thumbs-up or thumbs-down. Votes are visible to the group.
  • • Discover — vibe-matched popular activities for your destination. Tap any one to add it straight to the voting block.

No more scrolling back through three weeks of WhatsApp messages to find that restaurant Sarah suggested. Every idea has its own card, its own vote count, and its own clear status.

Pro tip

Don't drag things to Confirmed too early. Let activities sit in the voting block for a few days so quieter members of the group have time to weigh in. The votes do the consensus-building for you.

Step 4 See the trip come together, day by day

The Itinerary tab auto-builds from confirmed activities. Each day shows the date, the day-of-week, the weather forecast, a 24-hour timeline, and the activities slotted in.

Tap any day to add an activity, reorder the schedule, or block out time. Days you haven't planned yet show "Nothing planned yet" — no guilt, no empty white space. Total trip spend rolls up at the top so the group always knows where things stand.

This is the page everyone in the group opens on the morning of each trip day. One source of truth — what's happening, when, and what the weather's doing.

Built-in weather

The forecast appears on the day card itself, not in a separate weather app. If Day 3 is going to rain, you see it next to your hike before you commit — and can swap in an indoor activity from the voting block instead.

Step 5 Track expenses and settle up

Whenever someone pays for something, they tap + on the Expenses tab and enter the amount, what it was for, who paid, and how it splits — evenly across the group, or only across the people involved.

TRYPS keeps a running balance per person. When everyone's square — like in the screenshot below — you see All Settled with a green check. When balances exist, you see exactly who owes what to whom.

That's it. No spreadsheet. No exporting to Splitwise. No awkward Venmo chase a week after the trip ends.

Add expenses as they happen

The trick is to log expenses the moment they happen — not at the end of the trip. It takes about 10 seconds in the app vs. a stressful hour with a notebook on the flight home. Set the precedent on day one and the rest of the group follows.

Common mistakes to avoid

Coordinating across three apps

Chat for ideas, Sheets for itinerary, Splitwise for money. Every app split is a coordination tax. TRYPS replaces all three.

Skipping the vibe step

Two minutes upfront prevents the day-three argument about pace or budget. Don't skip it.

Confirming activities too early

Let votes accumulate for a few days. The quieter people in the group need time to weigh in.

Logging expenses at the end

Receipts get lost. Memories blur. Add each expense the moment it happens — it takes ten seconds.

Not naming a host

A trip without a clear owner drifts. The host doesn't have to decide everything — they just have to keep things moving. TRYPS makes the role official.

Frequently asked questions

Do my friends need to download TRYPS to join my trip? ▾

No. When you tap Text Friends, TRYPS sends an iMessage with a join link. Friends tap the link and land directly inside the trip — they can vote on activities, see the itinerary, and add expenses without installing the app first.

Is TRYPS free to use? ▾

Yes. TRYPS is free for the host and every guest. There is no paywall on creating a trip, inviting friends, voting on activities, building an itinerary, or splitting expenses.

How does group voting on activities work in TRYPS? ▾

Anyone in the trip can add an activity to the Voting Block. Each member casts a thumbs-up or thumbs-down. Vote counts are visible to the whole group, so it's clear when something has consensus. Drag a popular activity to Confirmed and it slots into the day-by-day itinerary.

How does TRYPS split expenses between friends? ▾

When someone pays for something, they add the expense, choose who paid and how it splits — evenly across the group or only across the people involved. TRYPS keeps a running balance and shows All Settled when nobody owes anyone money.

Can I plan multiple trips at the same time? ▾

Yes. Each trip is a separate space with its own people, activities, itinerary, and expense ledger. Switch between them from the home screen without anything overlapping.

What kind of trips work best with TRYPS? ▾

Anything that involves more than two people making decisions together — weekend getaways, bachelor and bachelorette trips, ski trips, group beach vacations, multi-city adventures. The bigger the group, the more friction TRYPS removes.

What's the best app for planning a group trip with friends? ▾

TRYPS combines iMessage invites, group voting, a shared itinerary with weather and timing, and built-in expense splitting in one place. That's the difference: friend groups stop bouncing between WhatsApp, Google Sheets, and Splitwise and coordinate the whole trip from a single app.
